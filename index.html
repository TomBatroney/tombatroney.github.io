Bio

Some of my fondest childhood memories growing up in Northeastern Pennsylvania were in the woods and next to water. Going on summer camping trips with my family, swimming, and fishing in the nearby streams.

After high school I would enroll in the Environmental Engineering program at nearby Wilkes University and would learn about the engineering that water, land, and air. Water always interested me the most of the three. The physics and movement of water, the chemical reactions that go into treating water. Going into my senior year my advisor would encourage me to apply to graduate schools. Luckily I landed a internship and scholarship at Villanova University where I studied at the Villanova Urban Stormwater Partnership as full time graduate student. There I further grew my water resources engineering knowledge in stormwater management, green infrastructure, data analysis, and hydrologic and hydraulic modeling theory.

After Villanova I would move to Pittsburgh to work in engineering consulting and start my professional career. I'm really still the kid who loves being outdoors and learning as much as I can about water systems.

Employment History

Senior engineer for AKRF's Pittsburgh office primarily focusing on stormwater management project delivery and business development initiatives in the Pittsburgh and Midwest region of the United States. Project highlights including leading the development of technical components of the City of Pittsburgh stormwater management code and ordinances updates and associated chapters of the Stormwater Management Design Manual.

Senior Principal Engineer, Mott MacDonald (2007 to 03/2020)

Like many other early career professionals, I started my career in the field. Work primarily included stream monitoring and sewer construction inspection oversight. After a couple years in the field I would transition to the office for design work including design of sewer collection systems, combined sewer structures, and pumping stations. Eventually I would use my hydrologic and hydraulic modeling and GIS skills for city scale sewer, storm, and water system planning work for several cities in the Midwest and Northeast portions of the country.
